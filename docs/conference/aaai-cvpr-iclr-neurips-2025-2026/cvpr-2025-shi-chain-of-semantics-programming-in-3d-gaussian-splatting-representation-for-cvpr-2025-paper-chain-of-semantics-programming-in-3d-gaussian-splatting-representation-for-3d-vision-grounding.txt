Chain of Semantics Programming in 3D Gaussian Splatting Representation for
3D Vision Grounding
Jiaxin Shi, Mingyue Xiang, Hao Sun, Yixuan Huang, Zhi Weng*
Inner Mongolia University

Abstract
3D Vision Grounding (3DVG) is a fundamental research
area that enables agents to perceive and interact with the
3D world. The challenge of the 3DVG task lies in under-
standing fine-grained semantics and spatial relationships
within both the utterance and 3D scene. To address this
challenge, we propose a zero-shot neuro-symbolic frame-
work that utilizes a large language model (LLM) as neuro-
symbolic functions to ground the object within the 3D Gaus-
sian Splatting (3DGS) representation. By utilizing 3DGS
representation, we can dynamically render high-quality 2D
images from various viewpoints to enrich the semantic in-
formation. Given the complexity of spatial relationships,
we construct a relationship graph and chain of semantics
that decouple spatial relationships and facilitate step-by-
step reasoning within 3DGS representation. Additionally,
we employ a grounded-aware self-check mechanism to en-
able the LLM to reflect on its responses and mitigate the
effects of ambiguity in spatial reasoning. We evaluate our
method using two publicly available datasets, Nr3D and
Sr3D, achieving accuracies of 60.8% and 91.4%, respec-
tively. Notably, our method surpasses current state-of-the-
art zero-shot methods on the Nr3D dataset. In addition,
it outperforms the recent supervised models on the Sr3D
dataset.
1. Introduction
3D Vision Grounding (3DVG) has been widely applied in
virtual reality (VR), the metaverse, and autonomous driv-
ing [25]. As a fundamental task of understanding spatial
relationships in 3D scenes and aligning them with human
natural language, it has recently received significant atten-
tion [24]. Since the representation of the 3D scene is often
based on the point cloud, which is semantically sparse and
subject to noise interference, the 3DVG task faces two main
challenges: acquiring fine-grained semantics and reasoning
about complex spatial relationships.

Some prior works have explored the introduction of 2D
information to gain extra semantics [4, 36, 39–41]. While
these methods demonstrate strong performance, they of-
ten rely on generating synthetic 2D images from the point
cloud, using real 2D images, or mapping 3D information
onto 2D space. The acquisition of real 2D images is lim-
ited, particularly from free-viewpoint perspectives, compli-
cating the alignment with 3D semantics referenced in utter-
ances [13, 14, 39]. Furthermore, the quality of 2D images
derived from point clouds is frequently low or incomplete,
hindering the extraction of clean, fine-grained semantics in
diverse scenes and also limiting the reasoning of spatial re-
lationships. Additionally, recent research aims to use pre-
trained foundation models, such as large language models
(LLMs) [18] and vision-language models (VLMs) [21, 22],
for understanding utterance, reasoning spatial relationships,
and facilitating zero-shot object grounding [36, 40]. While
these foundational models possess strong capabilities, they
often simplify complex 3D spatial relationships into basic
binary relationships and multiple relationships, such as the
distance between two chairs, the bed being to the left of the
sofa, and the table positioned between the sofa and the bed
[15, 16]. They only focused on object-centered relation-
ships, overlooking the connections between relationships
and the conditionality of those relationships. For example,

if we want to locate the largest window on the left side of the

capturing the connection and conditionality of spatial rela-

To solve these two problems, we propose a dynamic
zero-shot neuro-symbolic framework that integrates 3D and
high-quality 2D information to grounded reasoning, as
shown in Figure 1.
To improve spatial relationship rea-
soning, we propose the chain of semantics programming,
which is guided by the connection among relationships and
the conditionality of relationships. We first decompose the
utterance into a relationship graph as axillary 3D informa-
tion to extract the connection between key semantics and
spatial relationships and construct a chain of semantics to
split the utterance into multiple grounding steps to explic-
itly consider the conditionality of the relationships.
To achieve interactive dynamic reasoning between 3D
and 2D information through a chain of semantics, we lever-
age 3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) [11, 20, 35] to transform
raw 3D scenes into dynamic, interactive representations.
With 3DGS, we can render high-quality 2D images from ar-
bitrary viewpoints. Our framework explores the 3DGS rep-
resentation to infer the suitable observation viewpoint based
on 3D information and the utterance. It then renders the
corresponding 2D image and extracts fine-grained seman-
tic information from it. Subsequently, utilizing the 3D and
2D information, our framework can perform step-by-step
dynamic reasoning by the chain of semantics programming
and fine-grained semantics. Additionally, we incorporate a
grounded-aware self-check mechanism during the reason-
ing process, enabling autonomous reflection on the ground-
ing result and error correction. If the framework detects that
the grounding result is implausible, it will refer to the erro-
neous reasoning results and restart the reasoning process.
We evaluated our method using two publicly available
3DVG datasets: Sr3D [2] attained an overall accuracy of
91.4%, whereas Nr3D [2] achieved 60.8%. Our method out-
performed the recent zero-shot methodologies on the Nr3D
dataset and surpassed the performance of contemporary su-
pervised methods on the Sr3D dataset. Notably, the exper-
imental results indicate that utilizing 3DGS led to a 14.7%
improvement (View Dep.) in the chain of semantics pro-
gramming on Sr3D. This demonstrates that 3DGS can alle-
viate ambiguities caused by viewpoint changes and that the
fine-grained 2D semantics provided by 3DGS are benefi-
cial. Additionally, the chain of semantics effectively parses
the complex spatial relationships and provides suitable rea-
soning steps, especially when combined with programming.
Our contributions are summarized as follows:
• We propose a chain of semantics programming method
with the grounded-aware self-check mechanism for en-
hanced grounded reasoning in the 3DVG task.
• We introduce a semantic decomposition method that ex-
tracts key semantic relationships from natural language
utterances. This method constructs a relationship graph
and facilitates a chain of semantics programming, en-
abling multi-step object grounding.
• We first use 3DGS to reconstruct the 3D representation,
which enables interactive reasoning by integrating 3D in-
formation with high-quality 2D rendered images corre-
sponding to the observation viewpoints.
• Our method achieves 91.4% grounding accuracy on Sr3D
and 60.8% on Nr3D, surpasses prior zero-shot 3DVG
methods, and demonstrates performance comparable to
that of state-of-the-art supervised models.
2. Related Work
3D Vision Grounding.
Most methods for 3DVG adopt
supervised learning. A typical workflow involves pretrain-
ing a 3D classifier, such as PointNet++ [29], followed
by the integration of 3D spatial and linguistic features to
identify target objects [2, 6, 9, 16, 42].
Two key chal-
lenges in 3DVG are obtaining fine-grained semantic infor-
mation and reasoning about complex spatial relationships.
Some studies address the ambiguity in spatial relationships
by introducing viewpoint-related information [8, 13, 41].
Despite these advancements, such methods are often lim-
ited to the 3D modality, where the sparse and noisy point
cloud can hinder robust scene understanding [24, 39]. To
overcome the limitations of 3D data, some methods en-
hance semantic representation by incorporating 2D features
[4, 39, 41]. In addition, NS3D [16] and LARC [12] in-
troduce neuro-symbolic frameworks that encode spatial re-
lationships as neuro-symbolic programs to improve spa-
tial reasoning capabilities.
Recently, the robust reason-
ing abilities of the LLM have garnered significant atten-
tion, with several studies leveraging LLMs for zero-shot
object grounding [15, 17, 30, 33, 34, 36, 40].
For ex-
ample, ZS3DVG [40] combines 3D information and 2D
projections for visual programming. VLM-Grounder [36]
employs vision-language models based on 2D images for
reasoning and grounding the object. Our proposed neuro-
symbolic framework is also zero-shot.
However, unlike
previous methods, we achieve multi-step spatial reason-
ing by parsing the semantics of the utterance and utilizing
3DGS representation, which enables the simultaneous use
of viewpoint-related fine-grained 2D semantics and 3D se-
mantic information.
3D Gaussian Splatting.
3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS)
is a radiance field technique designed for rapid 3D scene
reconstruction [11, 20, 35]. Unlike Neural Radiance Fields
24561


(NeRF) [26, 28], which employs neural networks to map co-
ordinates to colors and densities, 3DGS represents scenes
through millions of 3D Gaussian kernels [20].
Given a
viewpoint’s pose matrix, 3DGS can render the correspond-
ing 2D image, achieving faster rendering speeds than NeRF
while maintaining image quality [11, 35]. Recently, 3DGS
representation has been increasingly applied in 3D vision.
For instance, GSSLAM [37] incorporates 3D Gaussians
for scene representation in Simultaneous Localization and
Mapping (SLAM) systems [19, 27], focusing on balancing
efficiency and accuracy, significantly enhancing map opti-
mization and re-rendering processes in RGB-D mapping.
GaussianNav [23], built upon 3DGS, introduces an innova-
tive map representation to enrich semantic information and
texture features within scenes. Gaussian-Det [38] models
objects continuously by treating input Gaussians as feature
descriptors across partial surfaces for 3D object detection.
In this work, we employ 3DGS to reconstruct 3D scenes,
rendering high-quality 2D images from inferred viewpoints
to capture fine-grained scene representations.
Visual Programming with Chain of Thought.
Visual
Programming is designed to generate executable code based
on visual information, serving as an explicit reasoning tech-
nique that enables LLMs to follow a programmatic Chain of
Thought, making the reasoning process both visible and in-
terpretable [10, 15]. Visual Programming has already been
applied in 3D visual reasoning. For example, NS3D [16]
utilizes CodeX [5, 7] to generate hierarchical programs,
while VISPROG [15] leverages in-context learning to create
high-level modular programs for handling complex, compo-
sitional natural language reasoning and image editing tasks.
ViperGPT [31] directly provides LLMs with API access
to modules, generating executable Python code for image
grounding. In this work, we utilize a chain of semantics
to guide code generation, facilitating multi-step spatial rea-
soning.
3. Methodology
In this section, we introduce our proposed zero-shot neuro-
symbolic framework that employs a LLM as a neuro-
symbolic function for object grounding. We first parse the
semantics of the utterance to extract the connections among
relationships and the conditionality of the relationships, as
shown in Figure 2 (a). We then reconstruct the 3D scene
using the 3DGS representation to enable exploration in 3D
worlds and render free-viewing 2D images, as shown in Fig-
ure 2 (b). Finally, this framework enables exploration of the
3DGS representation and utilizes a chain of semantics pro-
gramming to ground the object, as shown in Figure 2 (c).
To enhance the effectiveness and robustness of the program-
ming and reasoning process, we propose a grounded-aware
self-check mechanism that reflects on the reasoning results.

3.4. Grounded-aware Self-Check Mechanism
An error at any reasoning step can significantly affect the
final reasoning outcome. Additionally, the complexity of
multi-step reasoning increases the difficulty for LLM in
generating correct executable code. To address this, we as-
sess and validate the execution results by re-evaluating the
grounding outputs.
  \m a thcal {I}^{\text {err}} = \text {aware}(target) 
(14)
here, aware refers to a LLM module that returns the execu-
tion status of the code and analyzes the underlying causes
of any discrepancies. For instance, if the user intends to
locate a single object but two are returned, or if the execu-
tion yields no results (e.g., no object is located), or if syn-
tax errors are present in the generated code, the system can
identify such issues. This approach enables evaluation of
the reasoning results. If invalid, the grounding process is
re-executed:
  co d e = \ math cal {L}_p (\mathcal {U}, \mathcal {O}^{\text {3D}}, \mathcal {O}^{\text {2D}}, \mathcal {G}(\mathcal {U}), \mathcal {I}^{\text {err}}) 
(15)
where Ierr represents the error from the previous execution.
By incorporating the error message into the semantic pro-
gramming process chain, the same error can be prevented
in future executions.

Influence on Programming.
Programming improves ob-
ject grounding by 10.11% on the Nr3D dataset and 20.42%
on the Sr3D dataset, demonstrating its beneficial impact.
However, in Sr3D, the view-dependent grounding accu-
racy decreases by 12.8% (without Chain of Semantics) and
15.6% (with Chain of Semantics) compared to dialogue.
Conversely, significant improvements are observed in Easy,
Hard, and view-independent samples.
This may be at-
tributed to the template-based language of the utterances
in the Sr3D dataset, which lacks sufficient detail for view-
dependent descriptions. The use of programming for rea-
soning emphasizes the logical structure of spatial relation-
ships, resulting in a lower sensitivity to the ambiguous per-
spective relationships inherent in the utterances. This leads
to ambiguities when the LLM attempts stepwise reasoning
through programming. In contrast, the Nr3D dataset fea-
tures more freely constructed utterances, resulting in less
pronounced ambiguity due to perspectives than in Sr3D.
Influence on 3D Gaussian Splatting.
The use of 3DGS
enables the model to interact with 3D scenes, allowing the
LLM to infer fine-grained semantics from specific view-
points. The introduction of 3DGS enhances grounding ac-
curacy in both datasets, with improvements of 1.2% on
Nr3D and 3.5% on Sr3D. Notably, in Sr3D, 3DGS signifi-
cantly boosts the accuracy of view-dependent samples from
37.5% to 52.2%, representing an increase of 14.7%. This
underscores the importance of incorporating viewpoint-
related fine-grained semantics.
Influence on Grounded-Aware Self-Check Mechanism.
This mechanism achieves improvements of 4.5% on Nr3D
and 1.8% on Sr3D. In the context of programming, gen-
erating erroneous code is a common occurrence. Without
this mechanism, when errors occur during code execution,
the only option is to reattempt reasoning, failing to learn
from previous mistakes. This challenge is particularly pro-
nounced in Nr3D, where semantic complexity makes gen-
erating correct code more difficult. The introduction of this
mechanism enhances the accuracy of the generated code
and deepens the reasoning regarding spatial relationships

5. Conclusion
We present a zero-shot neuro-symbolic framework for 3D
vision grounding, utilizing a chain of semantics program-
ming in the 3D Gaussian Splatting representation to facil-
itate stepwise reasoning. Our method explicitly considers
the connections and conditionality of spatial relationships
and interacts with the 3D Gaussian Splatting representation
to render the corresponding 2D images from specific view-
points and extract fine-grained semantics.
Our approach
surpasses previous zero-shot methods and demonstrates
strong competitiveness when compared to current state-of-
the-art supervised models. We show that chain of semantics
programming enhances the understanding of complex spa-
tial relationships, and the 3D Gaussian Splatting represen-
tation provides fine-grained 2D semantics, overcoming the
limitations of reasoning based solely on 3D information.
